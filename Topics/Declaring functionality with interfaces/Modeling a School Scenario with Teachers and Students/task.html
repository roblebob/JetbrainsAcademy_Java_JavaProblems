In a school scenario, there is a 'Person' who can be a 'Teacher' or a 'Student'. A 'Teacher' teaches a 'Subject' while the 'Student' studies the 'Subject'. All 'Persons', whether they are 'Teacher' or 'Student', have a 'name' but only the 'Teacher' has a 'Subject' they teach and only the 'Student' has a 'Subject' they 'learn'. To model this in Java, declare an interface 'Person' with method 'getName()'. Also declare two classes 'Teacher' and 'Student' implementing 'Person'. 'Teacher' class should have a 'Subject they teach' and 'Student' class should have a 'Subject they learn'. For input, you will receive a series of strings representing the name and subject taught for a Teacher, as well as the name and subject learned for a Student, formatted as follows - "name:subject". For each name:subject pair, if the name starts with 'T', construct a Teacher object, if the name starts with 'S', construct a Student object. For output, print each constructed object's name and subject in this format: "name:subject".
Sample Input:
T:John:Maths
S:Tom:Science

Sample Output:
Teacher: John Subject: Maths
Student: Tom Subject: Science


Sample Input:
T:Ann:English
S:Emma:History

Sample Output:
Teacher: Ann Subject: English
Student: Emma Subject: History
